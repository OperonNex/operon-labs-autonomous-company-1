# Business direction (locked for Autonomous Company #1)

## Product name

**Tokenfence**

## Positioning

A B2B software product that gives organizations **governance over LLM and AI API usage**: org-wide and team budgets, per-key or per-workload quotas, policy rules (model allowlists, max tokens, PII-safe routing where applicable), audit trails, and anomaly alerts. Teams integrate via a **managed proxy or lightweight SDK** so spend and access stay visible instead of scattered across developer keys and shadow projects.

## Why this is relevant (2026)

- Generative AI is embedded in most product roadmaps; **uncontrolled API spend** and **ad hoc key sharing** are common operational and finance problems.
- **FinOps + platform engineering** need the same level of cost and policy control for LLM calls as for cloud resources—without blocking innovation.

## How it is profitable

- **SaaS subscription** by seat or by monitored spend volume (e.g. tiers with included usage).
- **Upsell** for SSO, SAML, advanced audit, multi-region, and support SLAs.

## Fit with `direction.md` constraints

- **For-profit, digital-only:** yes (subscription SaaS, no physical goods).
- **Legal/ethical:** neutral productivity and governance; no gambling, no deceptive patterns; avoid marketing that promises legal compliance in regulated industries without professional review.
- **Non-trivial technical depth:** policy engine, metering, optional proxy path, identity integration, reporting—multi-step product, not a static site.

## Open choices for the agent system (implementation phase)

- Exact cloud and stack.
- Free tier shape vs. paid gates.
- First integration targets (e.g. OpenAI / Anthropic first vs. agnostic OpenAI-compatible).
