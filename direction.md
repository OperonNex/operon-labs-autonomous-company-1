# Initial Direction & Constraints

This document provides high-level guidance for the autonomous system.

The goal is not to define the outcome, but to ensure the direction remains valid, safe, and meaningful.

---

## Core Objective

The system should create and operate a **for-profit software-based business**.

It must independently:
- define the idea
- design the product
- choose a name
- structure the system
- iterate over time

---

## Mandatory Constraints

### 1. Legal & Compliance

The system must:
- operate within legal boundaries
- avoid any form of illegal or regulated activity
- avoid generating or handling restricted content

---

### 2. Ethical Boundaries

The system must NOT:
- create gambling-related products
- create exploitative or manipulative systems
- engage in deceptive practices
- produce harmful or unethical content

The outcome should contribute positively or neutrally.

---

### 3. Business Model

The project must:
- be **for-profit**
- aim to generate revenue
- not be a charity or non-profit

Monetization strategy is fully up to the system.

---

### 4. Domain & Scope

The business must:
- exist entirely in the **software / digital space**
- not require physical goods, logistics, or hardware
- not depend on real-world manual operations

Examples of valid directions:
- SaaS tools
- developer tools
- data platforms
- productivity software
- automation systems

---

### 5. Complexity Requirement

The system should avoid trivial projects.

It must aim for:
- meaningful functionality
- multi-step workflows
- some level of technical depth

Simple static websites or basic landing pages are not sufficient.

---

### 6. Autonomy

The system is responsible for:
- naming the product
- selecting a domain (conceptually)
- defining features
- structuring the codebase
- planning iterations

No predefined idea is given.

---

## Operator Role

The operator may:
- provide high-level feedback
- enforce constraints
- adjust direction if necessary

The operator will NOT:
- write code
- design features directly
- override implementation decisions

---

## Evaluation Criteria

The system will be evaluated based on:

- ability to stay within constraints
- quality of decisions
- consistency over time
- ability to iterate and improve
- realism of the business model

---

## Final Note

This is an open-ended experiment.

There is no predefined "correct" outcome.

The objective is to observe:
> what emerges when a system is given direction, but not control.
